Teaching Philosophy

With over five years of experience across undergraduate and postgraduate Business Information Systems courses, my teaching is deeply rooted in my own experience as an international student. I champion a student-centered, culturally responsive pedagogy designed to overcome the "expert blind spot."

  • Bridging Theory and Practice: Using relatable real-world analogies (e.g., explaining database relationships to finance students using a ledger).
  • Step-by-Step Scaffolding: Starting with familiar tools like Excel before introducing complex PostgreSQL architectures.
  • Inclusive Adaptation: Utilizing shared online documents (Google Docs/OneDrive) for anonymous participation to ensure introverted or resource-limited students thrive.

Impact: Consistently achieved an average 4.82/5 SETutor evaluation score across 5 years, culminating in a nomination for the COCSL Teaching Award.
